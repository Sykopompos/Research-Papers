# Research Papers

Independent research papers applying a cross-system analytical framework to unsolved problems in biology and medicine. Each paper treats its subject as an integrated system — mapping the full cascade of mechanisms rather than isolating individual nodes — and derives testable predictions directly from the architecture.

Papers are posted as preprints. Not yet peer reviewed. If a journal wants to publish any of them, changes can be made at that point.

Markdown files are in /papers, these can be easily read here on github. Docx files are in `/docx` if you want to download them, just click view raw.

Summarized plain language versions are in the root folder.

---

## What this is

Most biomedical research is siloed. Oncology, immunology, neurology, endocrinology, and autonomic medicine all study the same body from separate windows and rarely synthesize across them. These papers are an attempt to do that synthesis rigorously — mapping complete biological cascades rather than individual pathway fragments, and identifying the upstream nodes that standard research and treatment leaves unaddressed.

More papers are in progress. They go up when they meet the same standard as what is already here: accurate, consistently cross-referenced, and properly cited.

## Disclaimer

Preprints — not peer reviewed, not clinical guidance, not medical advice of any kind. Every assertion is attached to its citation; if something doesn't hold up against the primary literature it gets corrected or cut.

I'm a self-taught systems architect, not a biologist or clinician. I read the literature, mapped the connections, and built the framework I needed.

**Tools used:** Primary writing and research synthesis via Claude.ai. Citation verification cross-checked against primary literature throughout. All AI-generated content is verified independently against primary sources — claims that do not hold up are corrected or cut. AI tools are prone to hallucination; the verification process is what separates this from generated slop. The work is mine — the tools make it possible to do it without an institution behind me.
